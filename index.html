The Theory Background

The Theory of Life originates from the famous Schrodinger's thougt experiment, that which is known as "The Schrodinger's Cat thought experiment".

The experiment has to do with a Cat's survival which is 50/50 chance. There's a 50% chance the cat will survive in the box and there's another 50% chance it will not.

If you're completely new to the theory i'ld recommend learning more about the Schrodinger's Cat thought experiment. As it'll be of extremely important to navigating and truly understanding the Theory of Life and what it stoods for.

Few months back, the creator of the proposed Theory was probably just pondering over the famous thought experiment... And he starts to wonder if there are some hidden gem underneath the thought experiment. But when he realized the box, how one can only find the truth if one opens the box... It clicked.

Instead of seeing a thought experiment, he saw an analogy of a newly created (Proposed) theory. The theory will have to be described and has to make a sense in one way or the other. The box is a mystery, so was our future. It's just unknown to us whether we'll be alive or dead, just like the cat. Putting one or two things together and making some adjustments based on people's feedback, He can finally said that this proposed theory does have a future.

The theory was greatly inspired by the schrodingers cat. In it's simplest form of analogy... Few questions are proposed. i) What if our future self is that in the box. But we don't just have one box, there are infinite number of boxes. But in each boxes we can be alive or dead, and we're traveling linearly along the box. Opening each one of the box as the present unravels. Hence we're aware of our current state. The state can only be two. It's either you're dead or alive. There are no other states. In our current world, you might thought we have other states e.g I'm so alive, I'm alive but i'm feeling great pain, Or I feel like a dead. First of all, the above list proves that you're alive. You should be grateful for that.

Why should you be grateful for that? Well, when experimenting with the theory we've come to a conclusion that best describes the analogy than a cat in a black box. Imagine a light bulb, the one our live met before coming back to us. If the light bulb is on, it means it should continue it's process. When it is off, it means it's time to leave the body. And this light bulb has a 50% chance of being off or on for every next minute.

But that still doesn't explain why we should be grateful. Listen to this, the light bulb doesn't have more chance of being on for you or being off for you. What'd i meant by this? Let's say i'm sick and on the verge of death, the light bulb doesn't have more percentage of being off to me... It's still 50% and 50%. In other words it remain constant.

You should be grateful for it because it could turn of any minute from now and it hasn't. That same time you're reading this, this light bulb has turned off to many lives that it was also previously On. It is wrong to think that you being alive is because you don't ate junk food, you're a vegetarian, you practice yoga every minute... or whatever it is that you do... This is what you do and you do it in preparating for the day it turns off. No matter what you do, you're not moving backward to the day it turn off, you're moving forward to the day it turns off.

So why should you be grateful? You're alive! Be grateful. But i'm alive because of this or that... That is a wrong way of thinking, you're not alive based on what you do or don't do, you're alive simply because in that present moment you're alive. And no matter what you do in order to prevent the inevitable for happening, it'll happen when it's time for every one of us light bulb to went off.

FAQ

Below are the common FAQ:

What are the benefit of this theory? Why was it created (proposed) in the first place?

The theory has a lot of what i'ld like to call "Advantage" or "Pros"... They include but not limited to:

#1

It let us live in the present moment. What does that mean? It means we only have one moment in life. We don't have the past for it is gone. We don't have the future for it is yet to come. But what do we have to live for? The Present.

This also leads us to being grateful of what we have. The present. And stop worrying about the past for it is gone. Or worrying about our mere speculation which is the future.

Living in the present might sounds great but it does have it's own shortcome. But imagine wasting that present moment, focusing on not it (the present), but on what it's gone or what it's yet to come.

As of writing, a book title comes to my mind "Your Future Starts Now". Which obviously decipate the illusion masses have over the future. You ask yourself, what did i want to do now? Maybe eat. Or maybe i'll eat in the next few minutes. Well, what's wrong with eating now... You're discussing about the next few minutes that have a 50% chance of caming into your existence or otherwise.

Living in the present moment let us cherish it, care for it, make thoughtful decision or choice - the one that will benefit us in the long run.

But here comes the weird part, if you only think about the present how do you make plans for your goals yet to come? Simple! You make plans just as normal, but also keep in mind it's not static yet. It hasn't happened. What you said you'll do in the next 5 days, you'll have to wait for the next 5 days before doing it... and live those moment (the next 5 days) presently rather than speculating or creating illusions of how you'll execute what you'll execute in the next 5 days.

#2

It leads to a peaceful coexistence. Imagine this, this moment might be the last moment of your life. So, ask yourself, if it was will i still be here with a gun in my hand hunting someone because of vengeance? Or will i be out here smoking marijuana? Ask deeply. If this moment is the last, is this how you'll spent it?

This might sound controversial, but think deeply about it... For starters, i used to think i have no heart, hence no love or hate... but thinking about this made me realize i wouldn't want to spend my last minute worrying about anything but instead doing the most right thing to do in that present moment. You also soon realize the most right thing to do has nothing to with inflicting pain for others, or raping someone, or burning the world.

If you think and think and nothing sort of peace came up, you're not thinking deeply. Not reaching the bottom of yourself. Deep down if it's really inevitable that next moment we'll be dead, we'll be at peace with others.

#3

It leads to Dettachment. I'm sort of a Buddhist guy. But we're not going down that road here. Dettachment in short means letting go. Letting go of the idea that the next minute is sure for you to be alive.

Seriously? Yeah. If you do think about letting go of the basic idea that you'll certainly be alive the next minute, the dettachment follows. The dettachment of possession, the dettachment of all; Imagine this, you'll die in less than 60seconds. You'll realize dettachment naturally follows. You stopped thinking of that certificate hung up in the room. You stopped basically attaching yourself to outside things. You're definitely going to spend the rest 59 seconds living as if you own nothing in life. Not even your house or car.

But what exactly is the benefit here? It leads to a world where people are not to attached with their possessions to the extent that they don't care about others in other to not loose that thing they're attached to. In reality, not only are they inflicting pain to others but themselves because one person hurt intentionally is you thinking about whether he/she will revenge in the future.
